# Design System

## Theme & Atmosphere
**Light Editorial / Compliance Meets Deep Research**
A quiet, focused environment that reduces cognitive load. The light mode evokes high-quality textured paper with dark charcoal ink. The dark mode feels like a sophisticated, low-light reading room (deep slate, not pure black).

## Color Strategy
**Restrained**
- Tinted neutrals + one accent color (≤10% of surface).
- Primary Accent: A muted but powerful color (e.g., a deep, intelligent crimson or a precise architect blue) for active states and graph highlights.
- Backgrounds: Warm ivory (light) or deep charcoal/slate (dark).
- Text: Off-black (charcoal) and off-white (soft cream) to reduce eye strain.
- Pure black (`#000`) and pure white (`#fff`) are banned.

## Typography
- **Headings**: A sharp, high-contrast serif to evoke editorial authority and permanence.
- **Body**: A highly readable, geometric sans-serif for comfortable long-form reading.
- **Code/Metadata**: A clean monospace for tags, dates, and code blocks.
- **Scale**: Large typographic contrast. Headings are significantly larger than body text. Line length capped at 65-75ch.

## Layout & Spacing
- Wide margins and generous negative space.
- Avoid nested cards. Use spacing and typographic hierarchy to separate sections.
- The graph visualization should feel integrated, not like a separate "widget".

## Details & Nuance
- Small, precise page numbers or node counts.
- Thin, delicate rules for separation (1px borders).
- Subtle, textured backgrounds (grain or paper feel) if performance allows.

## Motion
- Subtle, purposeful motion. Eased out transitions. No bouncy physics on UI elements (graph physics is an exception).
