# Sales Fundamentals - The Complete Guide

## The Sales Mindset

**Sales is not about convincing people to buy something they don't want.**
**Sales is about helping people solve problems they already have.**

## The SALES Framework

### S - Solution
Know what problem you solve and for whom.

### A - Approach
How you engage prospects - timing, channel, message.

### L - Listening
Understanding their world before offering solutions.

### E - Education
Teaching them something new about their situation.

### S - Solution (Offer)
Presenting your solution as the logical next step.

---

## The Sales Conversation Flow

### 1. Discovery (20% of time)
**Goal**: Understand their world

**Questions to ask**:
- "What prompted you to look into this right now?"
- "What's the biggest challenge you're facing with [topic]?"
- "What have you tried before?"
- "What's holding you back from solving this?"
- "What would success look like for you?"

**Listen more than you speak.** Take notes.

### 2. Diagnosis (30% of time)
**Goal**: Help them see the problem clearly

**Framework**: Problem → Impact → Root Cause

```
"I hear you saying [X]. That's causing [Y impact].
Most of our clients found that was actually because of [root cause].
How does that resonate with what you're experiencing?"
```

### 3. Prescription (20% of time)
**Goal**: Present your solution as the logical answer

**Don't list features.** Translate to outcomes.

```
WRONG: "Our system has XYZ features."
RIGHT: "Based on what you told me about [challenge],
        here's how this would help you [specific outcome]."
```

### 4. Objection Handling (20% of time)
**Most objections are actually questions in disguise.**

**Common patterns**:
- "It's too expensive" → "Help me understand the value"
- "I need to think about it" → "I haven't built enough trust yet"
- "Let me check with [person]" → "I don't have enough info to decide"

**The Feel, Felt, Found technique**:
```
"I understand how you feel. Others felt the same way.
But what they found was..."
```

### 5. Closing (10% of time)
**Closing should be the natural conclusion, not a battle.**

**Soft close**:
```
"Based on everything we've discussed, does this make sense
to move forward with?"
```

**Assumptive close**:
```
"Would you like to start on the 15th or the 22nd?"
```

**Option close**:
```
"Would you prefer the monthly or annual plan?"
```

## Qualifying: Don't Waste Time

### BANT Framework
- **Budget**: Can they afford it?
- **Authority**: Can they make the decision?
- **Need**: Do they actually need this?
- **Timeline**: When will they decide?

### Red Flags 🚩
- Won't give budget range
- Can't articulate their problem clearly
- "Just send me a proposal" (no conversation)
- Comparing against everything with no criteria
- Disrespectful of your time

**If you see 3+ red flags, disqualify politely.**

## Building Trust

Trust = Credibility × Reliability × Intimacy × Self-Orientation

### Credibility
- Show you understand their industry
- Share relevant case studies
- Demonstrate expertise without arrogance

### Reliability
- Do what you say you'll do
- Follow up when you say you will
- Be on time

### Intimacy (Closeness)
- Show genuine care
- Remember personal details
- Be human, not a salesbot

### Self-Orientation (Low is good)
- Focus on their success, not your commission
- Admit when your product isn't a fit
- Recommend competitors when appropriate

## The Follow-Up System

### Timeline
| Touch | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| Day 0 | Send meeting notes, next steps |
| Day 2 | Send promised resources |
| Day 5 | Check in, answer new questions |
| Day 14 | Value add, not just "any update?" |
| Day 30 | Monthly newsletter, industry insights |

### Value-Add Follow-Up Ideas
- Relevant article about their industry
- Introduction to someone in your network
- Case study of similar client success
- Invitation to webinar/event
- New feature that solves their specific problem

**Never follow up just to "check in."** Always bring value.
