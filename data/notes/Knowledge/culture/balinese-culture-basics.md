# 🌺 Balinese Culture Basics

> Understanding the rich traditions, customs, and philosophy of Bali.

---

## 🙏 Core Philosophy: Tri Hita Karana

The three causes of well-being:

1. **Pawongan** - Harmony with people
2. **Palemahan** - Harmony with nature/environment
3. **Parahyangan** - Harmony with the divine

This philosophy guides daily life, architecture, and community decisions.

---

## 📅 The Balinese Calendar System

### Pawukon Calendar (210-day cycle)

The Pawukon cycle consists of 30 weeks (wuku), each 7 days long.

**Important Wukus**:
- **Watugunung** - Last week, knowledge and learning
- **Sinta** - First week, new beginnings
- **Klungah** - Creation week

### Saka Calendar (Lunar year)

Used for temple anniversaries (Odalan).

---

## 🛕 Temple Types

### 1. Pura Kahyangan Jagad
Mountain temples, most sacred.
- Example: Pura Besakih (Mother Temple)
- Direction: Towards Mount Agung

### 2. Pura Kawitan
Ancestral temples, clan worship.
- Location: Village origin (Pura Puseh)
- Purpose: Ancestor veneration

### 3. Pura Segara
Sea temples, guardian of the land.
- Example: Tanah Lot, Uluwatu
- Direction: Towards the sea

---

## 🎭 Ceremonial Types

### Galungan & Kuningan
**Galungan**: Victory of dharma over adharma
- Occurs every 210 days
- 10-day celebration
- Penjor (bamboo poles) line the streets

**Kuningan**: Final day of Galungan
- Ancestral spirits return to heaven
- Offerings of yellow rice (kwijen)

### Nyepi
Balinese Day of Silence
- New Year (Saka calendar)
- Complete silence, no lights, no fire
- Ogoh-ogoh parades the night before

### Cremation (Ngaben)
- Release of the soul from the body
- Elaborate ceremony
- Bodies cremated to free the soul for reincarnation

## 🎨 Traditional Arts

### Dance
- **Baris** - Warrior dance
- **Legong** - Classical feminine dance
- **Kecak** - Fire and trance dance
- **Barong** - Mythical creature dance

### Music
- **Gamelan** - Traditional percussion ensemble
- **Gender Wayang** - Shadow puppet music
- **Angklung** - Bamboo instrument ensemble

### Crafts
- **Batik** - Wax-resist dyeing
- **Songket** - Brocade weaving
- **Silverwork** - Celuk village specialty
- **Woodcarving** - Mas village specialty

---

## 📜 Social Structure: Subak

Traditional water management system:
- Community-controlled irrigation
- Water temples coordinate water sharing
- Recognized by UNESCO
- Sustainable rice cultivation

---

## 🍛 Daily Offerings: Canang Sari

Small woven palm-leaf baskets with:
- Flowers (various colors have meanings)
- Rice
- Food
- Incense

Placed daily at:
- Temples
- Homes
- Workplaces
- Street shrines
