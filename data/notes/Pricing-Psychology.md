# Pricing Psychology - Value-Based Pricing

## The Core Principle

**Price based on value delivered, not hours worked.**

**Hourly pricing = Commoditization**
Value pricing = Premium positioning

---

## Value-Based Pricing Framework

### Step 1: Understand Their Problem

**The more painful the problem, the higher the value.**

**Problem intensity scale**:
- **Inconvenience**: Low value (IDR 500K - 2M)
- **Annoyance**: Medium value (IDR 2M - 8M)
- **Pain**: High value (IDR 8M - 25M)
- **Crisis**: Extreme value (IDR 25M+)

### Step 2: Quantify the Impact

**Help them see the ROI (Return on Investment).**

```
"Let's look at the numbers:

Without this solution: [Current state costs/problems]
With this solution: [Future state benefits]

Investment: IDR [Your price]
Expected return: IDR [Their gain]

Payback period: [Time to break even]

After that, it's pure profit."
```

### Step 3: Price Based on Impact

**Not what it costs you - what it's worth to them.**

| Scenario | Your Cost | Your Price | Margin |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Basic website for small cafe | 3M | 8M | 62% |
| E-commerce for growing brand | 8M | 25M | 68% |
| Custom system for established company | 20M | 50M+ | 60%+ |

**Your margin stays healthy** because you price on value.

## Pricing Psychology Tactics

### Anchor High

**Start with your premium offering.**

```
"We have three packages:

⭐ PLATINUM: Everything included - IDR 50M
  - Custom design
  - Full features
  - Priority support
  - Dedicated team

⭐ GOLD: Great value - IDR 25M
  - Template design
  - Core features
  - Email support
  - Shared team

⭐ SILVER: Essential - IDR 10M
  - Basic template
  - Core features
  - Community support

Which works for you?"
```

**Most choose Gold** - it feels like the middle option, but Platinum makes it look reasonable.

### The Rule of 100

**If it seems too expensive, break it down.**

```
"I understand IDR 15M feels like a lot.

But let's break it down:
- This website will serve you for [3+ years]
- That's IDR 416K/month
- Or IDR 13K/day

For a professional business presence
that brings you customers 24/7...

Is it still expensive?"
```

### Three Options Rule

**Always offer 3 options:**

1. **Good** - Basic, lower price
2. **Better** - Popular, middle price
3. **Best** - Premium, higher price

**Why works**:
- Gives them choice
- Makes comparison between your options (not competitors)
- Most choose middle (your target)

### Bundle for Higher Value

**Add related services at discount.**

```
"Base package: IDR 10M
- Website

Add-on packages (bundle for 20% off):
- SEO package: IDR 5M → IDR 4M
- Maintenance (6 months): IDR 3M → IDR 2.4M
- Copywriting: IDR 4M → IDR 3.2M

Full package: IDR 22M → IDR 17.2M
You save IDR 4.8M!"
```

## The Price Increase Script

### For Existing Clients

```
"Hi [Client Name],

Hope everything is going well with [Project]!

I'm writing to let you know about our 2026 pricing updates.

Due to [reasons - increased costs, added value, etc.],
our rates will be increasing by [X]% starting [date].

However, as an existing client, you're locked in
at current rates until [date].

If you'd like to lock in any additional work
at the current rate, let's discuss before [date].

I value your business and want to make sure
this works for you!"

The key: Lock them in before increase, they feel protected.
```

## Handling Price Objections

### "That's expensive!"

**Don't defend the price. Build the value.**

```
"I understand it feels like a lot.

Let me show you what you're getting:
[Break down everything included]

And what this enables for you:
[Specific outcomes/benefits]

Most clients see ROI within [timeframe].

How much would it cost you NOT to solve this problem?"
```

### "Can you give me a discount?"

**Trade, don't give.**

```
"I can offer a 10% discount for full upfront payment.

Or I can remove [X feature] to lower the price.

Which would you prefer?"
```

**Never discount without getting something in return**:
- Faster payment
- Longer commitment
- Reduced scope
- Case study permission
- Referral commitment

### "Your competitor is cheaper"

**Differentiate, don't compete on price.**

```
"They do offer a lower price. Here's what that usually means:
- [What they don't include]
- [Risks with cheaper option]

Here's what's different about our approach:
- [Your unique value]
- [Your process/quality]
- [Your support/reliability]

Is the lower price worth those risks?"
```

## Psychological Pricing Numbers

### The Power of 9
- IDR 9M feels cheaper than IDR 10M (even though 1M difference)
- IDR 19M feels cheaper than IDR 20M

### Round Numbers
- IDR 10M = round, negotiable
- IDR 9.7M = calculated, firm

### For Indonesian Market:
- IDR 15.000.000 = premium, firm
- IDR 14.750.000 = calculated
- IDR 15M = round number, friendly

## Pricing Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Common Mistakes

1. **Basing price on hours** = Commoditizes you
2. **Underpricing to "get the gig"** = Attracts bad clients
3. **Discounting too easily** = Destroys perceived value
4. **Not increasing prices annually** = Inflation eats margins
5. **Charging the same for everyone** = Some clients underpay, some can't afford

### ✅ Best Practices

1. **Price on value delivered** = Higher margins
2. **Have a minimum price** = Walk away from bad fits
3. **Discount strategically** = Always get something in return
4. **Raise prices annually** = Keep up with inflation/growth
5. **Tier your pricing** = Serve different segments

## Pricing Formula

### For Service Projects

```
BASE PRICE:
- Your desired monthly income × [months]
= Target annual revenue

PER PROJECT:
- Target annual revenue ÷ [projects you can handle]
= Average project price

ADJUST FOR:
- Project complexity
- Client size/ability to pay
- Market positioning
- Urgency/timeline pressure
- Your availability/desire

FINAL PRICE = Base × Multiplier
```

### Example Calculation

```
My goal: IDR 150M/year revenue
I can handle: 20 projects/year
Base price: IDR 7.5M/project

This client:
- Larger than average → ×1.5
- Urgent timeline → ×1.3
- Interesting work → ×1.1

Final price: 7.5M × 1.5 × 1.3 × 1.1 = IDR 16M

Round to: IDR 15M or IDR 17M for psychological effect
```
