# Follow-Up Systems - The Fortune is in the Follow-Up

## The Hard Truth

**48% of salespeople never follow up with a prospect.**
**80% of sales are made on the 5th to 12th contact.**

**Most people stop after 1-2 follow-ups.**
**This is your competitive advantage.**

---

## The Follow-Up Mindset

### It's Not "Checking In"
"Checking in" says "I need something from you."

Instead: **Always bring value.**

### The Value-Add Formula
Each follow-up should include at least one of:
- Relevant article/resource
- Introduction to someone helpful
- Insight about their industry
- Case study of similar success
- Answer to a question they raised

### The Rule of 7
Someone needs to see/hear your message 7 times before they remember you.

| Touch | Content | Goal |
|-------|---------|------|
| 1 | Initial meeting notes | Reinforce what was discussed |
| 2 | Promised resource | Reliability |
| 3 | Industry insight | Expertise |
| 4 | Case study | Social proof |
| 5 | Introduction | Network value |
| 6 | New feature/update | Freshness |
| 7 | Check-in | Relationship |

## Follow-Up Timelines

### After Initial Meeting (Service Business)

**Immediately (same day)**:
```
"Great meeting you today, [Name].

Here are my notes from our conversation:
[bullet points of what was discussed]

Next steps: [your action items] by [date].

I'll follow up on [day]."
```

**Day 2-3**: Send any promised resources
```
"Hi [Name], here's the [resource] I mentioned.

[link/attachment]

Let me know if you have any questions!"
```

**Day 7**: Value add, no pressure
```
"Hi [Name], saw this article about [their industry topic]
and thought of you.

[link]

No need to reply—just wanted to share."
```

**Day 14**: Question-based follow-up
```
"Hi [Name], how are things going with [project/challenge]?

Any new developments on [topic we discussed]?

Happy to help if you hit any roadblocks."
```

**Day 30**: Monthly value
```
"Hi [Name], hope you're doing well!

Just wanted to share: [client success story relevant to them].

Also, we've rolled out [new feature/service]
that might help with [their challenge].

Let me know if you'd like to catch up!"
```

**Then monthly**: Stay in their peripheral vision
```
"Hi [Name],

[Monthly update: one valuable thing]

No pressure—just thinking of you.
Best, [Your Name]"
```

## After Proposal Submission

### Day 0: Confirmation
```
"Hi [Name], proposal is attached!

[Quick summary: what they're getting, investment, timeline]

I'll follow up on [day] to answer any questions.
Excited to work together!"
```

### Day 3: Question, not pressure
```
"Hi [Name], did you have a chance to review the proposal?

Any questions or areas you'd like me to clarify?

No rush—just want to make sure you have everything you need."
```

### Day 7: Address the elephant
```
"Hi [Name],

I haven't heard back, which usually means one of three things:

1. You're still reviewing (totally fair)
2. You've gone in a different direction (no worries)
3. You have questions I haven't answered (happy to help!)

Which is it? I'd appreciate knowing either way."
```

### Day 14: The "break up" or "leave door open"
```
"Hi [Name],

I'm following up one last time about the proposal.

If the timing isn't right, I completely understand.
Would you mind if I checked back in [3 months]?

If you've gone another direction, I wish you the best!
Either way, it would be great to know."

**Then**: Move to monthly nurture if they don't respond.
```

## The "Staying Alive" System

### For Long-Term Prospects

People who said "not now" but might be interested later.

**Monthly newsletter**:
- One valuable insight
- One client success
- One company update
- One soft CTA at the end

**Format** (email or WhatsApp broadcast):
```
Hey [Name/Everyone],

This month: [one valuable tip for their industry]

Client spotlight: [How client achieved X]

Quick update: [What's new at your company]

If you're ready to move forward on [service],
just reply and we'll get you scheduled.

Best,
[Your Name]

P.S. Know someone who needs this? Forward this along!
```

## Automated Follow-Up Systems

### Using Your CRM or Tools

For [[Business-Entities]], set up:

**Trigger-based follow-ups**:
- When: [Event happens]
- Then: [Send this message]
- Wait: [Time period]
- If no response: [Send next message]

**Example**:
```
Trigger: Lead downloads PDF
→ Wait: 2 days
→ Send: "How was the PDF?" email
→ Wait: 7 days
→ Send: "Case study related to PDF" email
→ Wait: 30 days
→ Send: "Monthly newsletter" (ongoing)
```

### Calendar Reminders

For personal follow-ups (not automated):

**Weekly review** (every Friday):
- Who did I meet this week?
- What follow-ups are pending?
- Schedule next week's touches

**Monthly review** (last Friday of month):
- Which prospects need nurturing?
- Any "dead" leads to revive?
- Plan next month's value content

## Follow-Up Scripts by Situation

### "I never got back to you" (You ghosted them)
```
"Hi [Name],

I realized I never followed up from our last conversation.
That's on me—sorry about that!

Have you made any progress on [topic]?

If you're still interested in discussing [service],
I'd love to pick up where we left off.

If not, no worries at all!
Best, [Your Name]"
```

### "They went cold" (Stopped responding)
```
"Hi [Name],

I haven't heard back in a while, so I assume
[this isn't a priority anymore / you've gone in a different direction].

That's completely understandable!

I'll close your file for now, but if things change
and you'd like to reconnect, just reach out.

Wishing you all the best with [their goal]!"
```

### "They said no" (Follow-up anyway)
```
"Hi [Name],

I completely respect your decision to pass on [service].

In the meantime, I thought you might find this interesting:
[value-add content - article, case study, etc.]

No sales pitch—I just thought of you when I saw this.

Keep doing great work with [what they do]!"
```

## The Power of Personal Notes

### Remembering Details

Set up your CRM or notes system to track:

- Their company/role
- Personal details (kids, hobbies, interests)
- Pain points discussed
- Goals mentioned
- Objections they had
- Timeline/decision process

**Use this in follow-ups**:
```
"Hi [Name], how's [personal detail - e.g., the new restaurant opening]
going? Also, wanted to check in on [business topic]..."
```

This shows you care about them as a person, not just a sale.

## When to Stop Following Up

### The 3×3 Rule
- 3 meaningful touchpoints
- Over 3 months
- With zero response

**Then**: Move to passive nurture (monthly newsletter only)

### Exceptions (stop immediately)
- They ask you to stop
- They're rude/disrespectful
- They've clearly gone with a competitor
- Your gut says "bad fit"
