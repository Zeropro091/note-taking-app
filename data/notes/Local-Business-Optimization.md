# Local Business Optimization (Indonesia)

## The Key Insight

**Pricing Mismatch**: International pricing models don't work in Indonesia.

## The Problem

Western SaaS pricing:
- $29/month → ~IDR 460K/month
- $99/month → ~IDR 1.5M/month

Indonesian reality:
- Average salary: IDR 5-8M/month
- $29/month = 6-10% of monthly income
- **Too expensive for mass adoption**

## The Solution

### Pricing Strategy

#### For B2B (Small Businesses)
**Sweet spot**: IDR 100K - 500K/month
- Perceived as affordable
- Fits in operational budgets
- Low barrier to entry

**Example tiers**:
- Basic: IDR 99K/month
- Pro: IDR 249K/month
- Enterprise: IDR 499K/month

#### For B2C (Consumers)
**Micro-transactions**: IDR 10K - 50K
- One-time purchases
- "Top-up" model
- Bundle packages

### Payment Ecosystem

Indonesia doesn't use credit cards for small purchases.

**Use instead**:
- **GoPay** - Most popular
- **OVO** - Second largest
- **Dana** - Growing
- **ShopeePay** - Integrated with e-commerce
- **QRIS** - Universal QR standard

### Language & Culture

**Do**:
- Use Indonesian language
- Reference local culture
- Build for mobile-first (90%+ mobile usage)
- Integrate with WhatsApp (messaging = business)

**Don't**:
- Direct translate Western copy
- Assume credit card usage
- Ignore Islamic values (halal certification)

## Case Study: [[Photobooth-SaaS-Plan]]

**Adjusted pricing for Indonesia**:
- Event Pass: IDR 150K (not $10)
- Monthly: IDR 500K (not $30)
- Custom: Starting IDR 1M

**Result**: 10x more potential customers

## Marketing Channels

### What Works in Indonesia

1. **Instagram** - Huge for business discovery
2. **TikTok** - Growing rapidly for products
3. **WhatsApp Business** - Direct communication
4. **Tokopedia/Shopee** - E-commerce integration
5. **KOL (Key Opinion Leaders)** - Local influencers

### What Doesn't Work

- Google Ads (too expensive)
- Facebook Ads (declining effectiveness)
- LinkedIn (B2B only, limited reach)
- Email marketing (not culturally adopted)
