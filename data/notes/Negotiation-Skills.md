# Negotiation Skills - Win-Win Agreements

## The Negotiation Mindset

**Good negotiation isn't about winning at the other person's expense.**
**Good negotiation is finding the maximum win-win.**

If they feel like they lost, they'll sabotage the implementation later.

---

## Preparation: Before You Negotiate

### Know Your BATNA
**BATNA** = Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement

**What will you do if this deal falls through?**
- Another prospect in pipeline?
- Different offering you can propose?
- Walking away is better than bad deal?

**Your BATNA = Your power.** The stronger your BATNA, the less you need this deal.

### Know Their BATNA (Approximately)
**What are their alternatives?**
- Competitors they're considering?
- DIY option?
- Doing nothing?
- Timeline pressure they're under?

**Ask questions to uncover**:
```
"What other options are you considering?"
"What happens if you don't solve this?"
"What's your timeline for making a decision?"
```

### Know Your Walk-Away Point
**The worst deal you'll accept.**

Write it down before negotiating. Stick to it.

**Example for service business**:
```
Walk-away: IDR 3M minimum for this project
Why: Below that, my margin is too thin
```

### Know Your Zones

| Zone | Description | Your Approach |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Target** | Your ideal outcome | Start here, aim high |
| **Acceptable** | Still good, not ideal | Can live with this |
| **Walk-away** | Unacceptable | Polite decline |

## During the Negotiation

### 1. Listen More Than You Talk

**80/20 rule**: Listen 80%, talk 20%.

**Why**: You learn their:
- Real constraints
- Hidden motivations
- Flexibility on price vs. features
- Decision-making process

### 2. Anchor Strategically

**Whoever makes the first offer sets the range.**

**If they ask your price first**:
- Aim high but defensible
- "It depends on scope, but similar projects have been IDR 15-25M."

**If they anchor first**:
```
"We're thinking around IDR 10M for this."

Your response: "What does that scope include?"
(Then unpack the scope—usually they're comparing apples to oranges)
```

### 3. Trade, Don't Give

**Never give without getting.**

```
THEM: "Can you come down on price?"
YOU: "I could flex on price, but I'd need to adjust the scope.
       What's more important: budget or full functionality?"
```

**Things you can trade**:
- Price ↔ Scope (less work, lower price)
- Timeline ↔ Price (faster = more, slower = less)
- Payment terms ↔ Price (upfront = discount)
- Support level ↔ Price (tiered options)
- Exclusivity ↔ Price (they only work with you)

### 4. The Flinch

React visibly to their first offer.

```
THEM: "We can only pay IDR 5M."

YOU: *[Pause, look surprised/concerned]*
    "Hmm... that's quite a bit lower than we discussed.
    Let me think about what I can do at that price point."
```

**Why**: Creates doubt about their anchor. Shows your price has reasons.

### 5. Ask "Why?" Before Responding

```
THEM: "That's too expensive."

YOU: "Help me understand: compared to what?
     Is it your total budget, or just for this project?
     What were you expecting to invest?"
```

**Often**: They're comparing apples to oranges. Clarify before conceding.

## Common Negotiation Scenarios

### "We have a lower quote"

**Don't match immediately.** Understand the difference.

```
"I appreciate you sharing that.

Can I ask what their proposal includes?
[Listen carefully]

I see the difference. Their proposal doesn't include:
- [List what you have that they don't]

For that additional value, would you agree the difference is justified?

Or would you prefer I remove [X] to match their price?"
```

### "We need to think about it"

**They might be stalling.** Test their interest.

```
"I understand.

Just to help me plan: What would need to be different
for you to say yes today?

Is it the price? The timeline? Something else?"
```

**If they give a specific concern**: Address it, then trial close.

**If they stay vague**: They might not be serious. Move on.

### "Can you do it for [low price]?"

**Your response**:
```
"I appreciate you asking!

At [low price], I'd be losing money on this project.
That wouldn't be fair to either of us—I'd deliver
lower quality work, and you'd be unhappy with the result.

However, I could do [reduced scope] for that price.
Would that work?"
```

**Or**: Offer payment terms instead.
```
"I can't lower the price, but I could do this:
50% now, 50% on delivery in 60 days.

That might help with your cash flow—would that work?"
```

### "We need it by [impossible deadline]"

**Your response**:
```
"I understand the urgency.

To deliver by [impossible date], I'd need to:
- Bring in additional help (+cost)
- Work overtime (+cost)
- Delay other projects (+opportunity cost)

That would be [higher price].

Alternatively, we could deliver [core features] by [later date]
and the rest by [later date].

Which matters more: speed or full functionality?"
```

## Closing the Negotiation

### Summarize Before Committing

Never leave with ambiguity. Summarize:

```
"Just to confirm, we're agreeing to:
- [Scope/What you'll deliver]
- [Timeline/When you'll deliver]
- [Price/How much and when]
- [Payment terms/How they'll pay]

Did I miss anything?"
```

### Get It in Writing

Even for smaller deals:

```
"Great! I'll send over a simple agreement/summary email
confirming everything we discussed.

Once you reply with your confirmation,
we'll get started on [date]."
```

## Negotiation Ethics

### What's Fair
- Charging for the value you provide
- Making a reasonable profit
- Walking away from bad deals
- Protecting your interests

### What's Not Fair
- Taking advantage of their desperation
- Changing terms after agreement
- Delivering less than promised
- Misrepresenting your capabilities

### Your Reputation Matters

**Bali/Indonesia business community is small.** (See [[Business-Entities]])

How you negotiate spreads fast.
- Be fair but firm
- Keep your word
- Never burn bridges

### Cultural Considerations

**Relationship-first**:
- Build trust before negotiating
- Small talk matters
- Family connections = trust

**Face-saving**:
- Never put them in a corner publicly
- Let them feel like they "won" something
- Preserve harmony even while disagreeing

**Price sensitivity**:
- Focus on value, not just price
- Offer payment flexibility
- Consider smaller initial projects to prove value

## Practice Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Price Objection
```
Client: "IDR 15M is too much. Can you do 10M?"

You: "I understand budget is a concern.

At 10M, I'd need to reduce the scope by removing
[X feature] and [Y feature].

Those are important for [goal they mentioned].

Would you prefer the full solution at 15M,
or the reduced scope at 10M?

Or we could do 12M with 60% upfront and 40% on delivery."
```

### Scenario 2: Timeline Pressure
```
Client: "We need this in 2 weeks."

You: "I appreciate the urgency.

To deliver quality work in 2 weeks, I'd need to:
- Work exclusively on your project (+premium)
- Hire an additional developer (+cost)
- Skip some testing phases (-risk)

This would be 20M instead of 15M.

Alternatively, we could deliver [core features] in 2 weeks
and the rest by [later date] for 15M.

Which approach do you prefer?"
```

### Scenario 3: Scope Creep
```
Client: "Can you add [X] too? It shouldn't take long."

You: "I understand you need [X].

To add that properly, it would take an additional [time]
and cost an additional [price].

However, I could include a simplified version of [X]
at no extra cost if it helps.

Which would you prefer: full [X] for [price],
or the basic version included?"
```
